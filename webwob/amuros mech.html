Designing a KRSBiB Soccer Robot in CAD

From Concept to Manufacturing-Ready Design ⚽

Building a competitive robot doesn’t start on the field—it starts in the design stage. This project focuses on the 3D modeling and technical documentation of a KRSBiB (Kontes Robot Sepak Bola Indonesia Beroda) robot using professional CAD tools.

The objective is to produce a design that is not only visually complete, but also fully ready for fabrication, assembly, and real-world testing.

🧩 From Concept to Complete Assembly

Using SolidWorks, the robot was designed from individual components up to a full assembly, ensuring proper fit and functionality.

  • Structural frame (chassis)
  • Wheel and drivetrain mounting
  • Motor brackets
  • Sensor holders
  • Kicking mechanism for ball interaction

All parts were assembled digitally to validate alignment, motion, and mechanical compatibility before fabrication.

🛠️ My Role in the Project

I was responsible for the full mechanical design and technical documentation process, ensuring the system is manufacturable and reliable.

📐 Mechanical Design & 3D Modeling

Designed all major components with a focus on structural strength, efficient space utilization, and compatibility with motors and sensors.

🔩 Assembly Design

Built a complete assembly model to verify clearances, motion, and proper component integration before physical production.

📄 Technical (Engineering) Drawings

Created detailed drawings for parts and assemblies, including dimensions, tolerances, and material specifications to support manufacturing.

🎯 Key Learning Outcomes

  • Practical experience in mechanical design and CAD modeling
  • Understanding of design for manufacturing (DFM)
  • Exposure to system integration (mechanical + electronics)
  • Importance of accurate technical documentation

🚀 Why This Project Matters

  • ✅ Produces a complete design ready for fabrication
  • ✅ Reduces errors before physical manufacturing
  • ✅ Bridges concept design with real robotic implementation
  • ✅ Supports development of competition-ready robots

🌟 Final Thoughts

This project highlights how critical the design phase is in robotics. A well-developed CAD model doesn’t just represent a robot—it defines how efficiently it can be built, assembled, and maintained.

By combining detailed modeling with precise documentation, the result becomes a solid foundation for turning concepts into a functional, competition-ready system.
